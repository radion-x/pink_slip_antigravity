Our Blue Slip Services

We provide comprehensive AUVIS inspections for all light vehicles.

Expired Registration

If your rego has lapsed for more than 3 months, you need a Blue Slip to re-register. We check safety and identity.

Interstate Transfers

Moved to NSW? You must get a Blue Slip to register your interstate vehicle in New South Wales.

Defect Clearance

Got a "canary"? We inspect and clear defect notices issued by Police or Transport for NSW.

Identity Checks

We verify VINs, engine numbers, and vehicle history to ensure the car isn't stolen or written off.

When Do You Need A Blue Slip?

A Blue Slip is mandatory for unregistered vehicles in these situations.

Registration Expired

If your vehicle registration has been expired for more than 3 months, a Pink Slip is not enough.

Interstate Transfer

Bringing a vehicle from VIC, QLD, or another state? You need a Blue Slip to register it in NSW.

No Number Plates

If your vehicle has no plates or they were surrendered, a full inspection is required.

Defect Notice

Clear "yellow sticker" defect notices issued by Police or RMS/Transport for NSW.

Selling Unregistered

You must provide a Blue Slip inspection report to sell an unregistered vehicle.

Written-Off Repair

Re-registering a repairable write-off requires a comprehensive Blue Slip identity check.

Blue Slip FAQs

Everything you need to know about inspections.

What is the difference between a Blue Slip and a Pink Slip?

A Blue Slip (AUVIS) is for unregistered vehicles, interstate transfers, or cars with registration expired >3 months. It is a comprehensive safety and identity check. A Pink Slip is a lighter annual safety check for currently registered cars.

How long is a Blue Slip valid for?

A Blue Slip report is valid for 42 days (6 weeks) from the date of issue. You must complete your registration with Service NSW within this timeframe.

Can I drive my unregistered car to get a Blue Slip?

Yes, but strictly only for the purpose of getting the inspection or going to Service NSW. You must take the most direct route and cannot stop for other errands. We recommend booking first so you have proof of appointment.

What if my car fails the inspection?

If you fail, you have 14 days to repair the defects. If you return to us within 14 days for a re-inspection, no additional inspection fee is charged.

Do I need a Green Slip too?

Yes. To register your vehicle, you will need the passed Blue Slip (safety) and a Green Slip (CTP Insurance). Service NSW requires both.
